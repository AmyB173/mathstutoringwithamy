Parents FAQ

Have I left it too late to help them?

If they're in their first year of A Level (or just starting second) then NO, but you do need to make sure they're solid on the first year content ASAP. Be kind to youself - see my answer to "Should I have spotted it sooner?"

What is the best and quickest way for them to improve?

Make sure they are being thorough in their revision. My ethos is "back to basics" because if they don't have a solid foundation, everything else will feel shaky at best. They need to go back through the textbook, revise the concepts and ask themselves "Do I really understand what each part means? This is how I've designed my FULL A Level Rescue course - we cover all the essential Year 1 content so they can feel confident tackling the harder Year 2 content. If you might be interested in this, and want a free consultation on how your child can improve, then book a call

When is the best time to get a tutor?

Now. If you're already worried about their A Level progress, early intervention is always better. If they can't get really secure early on, any of the harder learning in the second year will just feel like an uphill battle!

Should I have spotted this sooner?

No! It is so hard to know as a parent, whether their struggles are manageable and they can manage them independently, or whether they need strong intervention to get them through. Be kind to yourself, you've spotted it now, and NOW is always a good time to start helping.

Should they be doing more past papers?

They SHOULD NOT be doing exam questions until the foundation is solid. Expert athletes spend most of their time NOT doing the event they're training for but instead focussing on the little skills that need honing, in small drills. It's the same here. Get all the concepts solid first, then you can truly make the most out of past paper practice.

What should I do if I feel the school has let them down?

Firstly, talk to them. Open a dialogue and explain your concerns calmly. You might be surprised at what they can offer. Saying that, schools and teachers are busier than ever, and it may be the case that your child isn't getting enough support. This is when you need to take it into your own hands. If you can afford to do so, get a tutor. But please make sure they have a plan. "The teacher explains things too quickly..."

Yes, they probably do. There is so much to get throuh in A Level. This is why it's so important to find things out of class that can break it down for you - friends, other teachers, a tutor, videos online.

"I feel like I faked a good grade at GCSE"

Even the most successful people suffer with imposter syndrome. Google celebrities with imposter syndrome and you will see. Just know, you didn't fake it, and you can get over this hurdle

"My teacher has given up on me getting a good grade ..."

In my experience, it's likely to teacher is just super busy and that's why they don't seem supportive. However, every student-teacher relationship is different and if you don't feel comfortable with yours it's so important to find a support network outside of the classroom to help you - whether that be friends, other teachers, a tutor or an online community.

Common feelings during the first year of A Level Maths

"It's easy in class but then the questions seems so much harder when I try them alone"

Yes. This is normal! I'm currently learning how to code and I rarely feel I can tackle a real challenge independetly right after being taught it. Usually, I need to go away and work on it independently for a while before I feel confident. I'm never afraid to ask for help, whether that be friends, tutors or youtube videos. Try to accept this and get help whenever you need it.

"I'm embarassed about my test grades"

Tests are funny things - they make us feel as though that's the representation of all our skill and learning. This really isn't true, they are a brief snapshot at best. Just know, if you go back to basics and solidify the core concepts, the test grades will follow. Saying that, peer and community pressue can be strong. Make sure to surround yourself with positive people and people in a similar situation to keep your spirits up.

"I don't like to ask for help"

I know, it's hard! But don't put it off, it's so important to get help. Try to find someone you're really comfortable with and start there, and then work your way up to asking a teacher in class.
